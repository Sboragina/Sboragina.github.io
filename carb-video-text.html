Video: What is carb counting?

I'm going to quickly demonstrate the steps involved in counting carbohydrates. I count carbohydrates to determine how much insulin to take to cover my food. It's a lot of math!

Here's how it works.

First, I plan what I'm going to eat:

  • two slices of toast,
  • a bowl of oatmeal, and
  • a banana.

I measure out my food. If the foods are ones I eat often, I likely already know what a serving size looks like and the corresponding carb count.

  • Nutrition facts for bread: 2 slices is 25 g of carbohydrates
  • Nutrition facts for oatmeal: one-half cup oatmeal is 33 g of carbohydrates
  • Food scale for banana: 29.43 g of carbohydrates

I calculate the total number of carbohydrates in the food.

Total carbohydrates: 87 g

I use my insulin-to-carb ratio to determine how many units of insulin I will need. An app or the bolus calculator on my insulin pump can help.

For an insulin-to-carb ratio of 1-to-15, that 87 g of carbohydrates requires 5.8 units of insulin.

If I'm feeling really disciplined, I wait about 15 minutes before I start eating my food so the insulin has time to travel to my bloodstream before I eat my food. I need to make sure I don't forget to eat and that nobody steals my food!
