6. Type 1 Diabetes:
Final Quiz

  1. People with type 1 diabetes

People with type 1 diabetes must inject insulin into their bodies multiple times a day to manage their blood sugar levels. Their bodies do not produce sufficient insulin for them to survive. Without injecting insulin, they will die. Those with type 2 diabetes may take pills and/or insulin to manage their blood sugar levels; some may be able to reverse it by improving their diet and activity levels.

  2. Pam is at a wedding. She has type 1 diabetes and has been following Dr. Bernstein's low-carb diet, but decides to eat the food provided and have a piece of wedding cake. In front of the wedding guests, an aunt squeals at her, "You know you shouldn't be eating that!" Pam is obviously upset. What is the best response to the aunt you could say to support Pam?

    Feedback: Option a is best. It shows you respect Pam's ability to take care of herself and that the other person needs to drop the topic. Option b makes the assumption that Pam shouldn't have as much cake as everyone else is having. Only Pam has the right to decide what she should have. Option c is just rude and will likely escalate the situation.

  3. You have two friends with type 1 diabetes, Rana and Jess. Rana is trying a new whole-foods plant-based diet to deal with her stage 3 kidney disease. When you mention this to your friend Jess, she insists that the keto diet is best for those with type 1 diabetes and says that she has achieved excellent blood sugar control since starting. What do you do?

    Feedback: Option b is best. Different diets may work best for different people. Sharing information may be helpful if you present it as an option to consider and not a cure or solution.

  4. After dancing and drinking all night long, Sarah, who has type 1 diabetes, staggers across the room, collapses in a chair, and goes to sleep. What should you do?

    Feedback: Option b is correct. Sarah has been drinking and dancing. Both activities typically lower blood sugar, so she likely has low blood sugar. Glucagon is not effective when someone has been drinking (the liver is busy processing the alcohol), and insulin will only make the situation worse. The best option is to bring her orange juice and her meter so she can confirm her blood glucose level and treat her low blood sugar herself. If she won't wake up or is unresponsive or combative, call 911 for assistance.

  5. Greg has been sick with a stomach flu, so he hasn't been eating. He hasn't been taking his insulin, either, because he's been worried about dropping low. He has started complaining that he hurts, and he's comiting every 10 minutes. What should you do?

    Feedback: Option c is correct. If ketones are present and high, take him to the ER. He is likely in DKA and needs immediate medical care to maek sure he gets the electrolytes he needs and the insulin he needs at an appropriate rate.

  6. Ricardo's brother Pablo admits that he only takes his insulin when he feels really horrible. What is a good response for Ricardo?

    Feedback: Option c is correct. If ketones are present and high, take him to the ER. He is likely in DKA and needs immediate medical care to maek sure he gets the electrolytes he needs and the insulin he needs at an appropriate rate.

  7. Soraya, who has type 1 diabetes, starts to cry and panic and says she can't see. What's the first thing you should do?

    Feedback: Option a is correct. The emotional reaction and the loss of sight are likely due to low blood sugar. Her body is directing glucose away from the eyes to support the core body functions.

  8. Your friend Jasmin has type 1 diabetes and is unconscious on the floor. In which of the following situations should you give her insulin?

    Feedback: Option c is correct. Even if you know her blood sugar is high, you likely don't know why she's unconscious and what is an appropriate dose of insulin to give to bring her blood sugar back down safely. Call 911 instead.

  9. Your sister tells you that your neice Olivia's teacher has sent home a message that Olivia is leaving class to use the bathroom at least once an hour. Your sister is planning to bring Olivia to the doctor to get her tested for a UTI. What is another test you should suggest she request?

    Feedback: Option a is correct. A symptom of high blood sugar (i.e., diabetes) is frequent urination. The test is cheap and easy, so it would be good to request.

  10. Michelle is baking cookies and realized she needs vanilla extract, but there's none in the house. She is about to ask Vince to quickly drive to the store and back to get her some more when she hears his cgm make a low blood sugar alarm, which means his blood sugar is 3.8 mmol/L or lower (really low). What should she do?
